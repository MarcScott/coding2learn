Personal Profile

For twelve years I was engaged as a passionate teacher in the disciplines of Science, ICT and Computing, before venturing into the world of EdTech with the Raspberry Pi Foundation. I've written a book to introduce children to coding, written projects for the Raspberry Pi Projects site, our magazines, The Scout Association and the European Space Agency.

I've trained teachers in the UK, Ireland and the USA, as part of the Foundation's Picademy program, as well as writing online courses for teachers.

I set up a pilot program with the National Citizen Service, to roll out physical computing as part of their program, and delivered the first set of workshops, prior to the scheme being rolled out nationwide over the past year.

I've worked in a variety of schools; as a teacher, a Curriculum Leader and a Network Manager, and have always been rated Outstanding by OfSTED and my line managers.

I am a confident public speaker, and as well as delivering training sessions to teachers, I have spoken at numerous events including delivering the closing Keynote Address at GitHub Satellite UK

I'm as comfortable in the Terminal as I am in the Classroom, and believe that both software and education should be free and open source.

I'm an occasional blogger, and ramble at coding2learn.org. I've had the pleasure of hitting the front page of Hacker News and Reddit.
